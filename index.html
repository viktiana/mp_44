Good ending

Leave

In the final scene, James encounters Maria in the form of Mary. He tells her that he no longer needs her and wants to put an end to this nightmare. The girl, however, wishes to take Mary's place by his side, but soon realizes that he, too, deserves to die. After the battle, the protagonist finds himself by Mary's bedside. He asks her to forgive him for what he has done, saying that he hated her because of her illness and wanted to regain a peaceful life. Mary asks why he is sad if that is true. She wants James to continue living and gives him a farewell letter, after which she dies. In the end, the hero leaves the city with Laura through the cemetery, while Mary reads her letter off-screen.

Bad ending

In Water

I In the final scene, James encounters Maria in the form of Mary. He tells her he no longer needs her, at which point she transforms into a demon and attacks him. After the fight, the protagonist finds himself next to Mary, who is lying in bed. He asks her to forgive him for what he has done, and Mary replies that she herself wanted to die to end the pain. She forgives James and gives him a letter. She begins to cough violently, and gradually falls silent. James takes her body with him to his car and drives into Lake Toluca. He decides to take his own life so he can be with his wife after death.

Bad ending

Maria

In the final scene, James meets Mary. She condemns him for everything he has done, transforms into a demon, and attacks him. After killing his wife for the second time, the protagonist returns to the waterfront, where he meets Maria and asks her to leave town with him. Maria gives him a letter from Mary, in which Mary forgives him and gives him permission to start a new life. On the way to the car, Maria starts coughing.

Strange ending

Rebirth

In the final scene, James encounters Maria in the form of Mary and declares that it's over between them. After the final battle, the hero rows a boat across Lake Toluca with his wife's corpse toward an island where a church is visible. It appears he intends to perform a dark ritual to resurrect Mary.

Strange ending

UFO

A large number of flying saucers swoop into the hotel room. Harry Mason, the protagonist of the first Silent Hill game, appears and asks Sanderland if he has seen a small girl with black hair. In response, James asks Harry if he has met his wife, Mary. Suddenly, an alien attacks James and, together with Harry, carries him unconscious onto one of the ships, after which all the UFOs fly away. This is a traditional humorous UFO ending; in this case, it is styled after a silent black-and-white film and is available only in special editions of the game.

Strange ending

Dog

James finds himself in a room fully equipped with computer equipment. A dog is sitting on a chair, pulling on some levers. With a cry of “So this is all your doing!”, the protagonist collapses to the floor. The dog runs over and starts licking his face.
